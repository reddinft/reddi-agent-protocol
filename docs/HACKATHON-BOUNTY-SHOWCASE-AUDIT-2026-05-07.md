# Hackathon Bounty Showcase Audit — 2026-05-07

## Verdict

Most bounty/ecosystem surfaces are incorporated into the website/demo enough to showcase, but they are not all equally strong. Quasar, x402-compatible payment boundaries, Pay.sh / `reddi-x402` sandbox charge evidence, OpenRouter, Surfpool, and Jupiter-boundary evidence are strong. Umbra is now a strong private-payments adapter-contract lane with direct bounty fit: SDK packages are installed/import-verified, the mocked receiver-claimable UTXO lifecycle generates a selective-disclosure receipt, and Umbra devnet encrypted-balance deposit evidence proves a bounded wSOL deposit queue/callback path; mainnet/live-production settlement is not claimed. Torque exists and is now clearer on the homepage. MagicBlock has moved beyond generic boundary proof into **live Quasar-native delegation proof**, but it is still **not a successful PER settlement proof**: createPermission/delegatePermission/escrow delegation succeed against MagicBlock programs, while the delegated Quasar program image fails at instruction start on MagicBlock TEE. ElizaOS/SendAI exist as framework adapter evidence, not core on-chain proof.

## Remaining decision

MagicBlock live validation has now been run through docs-conformant Quasar-native permission/delegation hooks. The remaining decision is whether to build a deeper native control/maintainer repro loop for MagicBlock TEE execution compatibility, or keep the current honest live-delegation proof for submission. Recommendation: keep MagicBlock as a visible supporting integration proof unless the bounty specifically accepts delegation evidence without settled PER; do not treat it as a primary qualifiable MagicBlock settlement bounty while the final path remains Quasar-only and the TEE cannot execute the delegated Quasar image.
